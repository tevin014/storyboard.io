They say war is a dance of glory. Glory of banners, kings, and for God

I've watched men scream for gods who never answered

Seeing your friend gurgle his last breath before you-the blood of the innocent and guilty spilled for kings in the name of protecting the weak. They chant Victory. But this doesn't feel like triumph.

EMPTY.

EMPTY.

As sworn captain of this Holy Order, I pledged to serve and protect and all my lifetime, I've done just that. I've led my men to victory, to death . But this time, something in my bones feels wrong. Like the silence before a storm, a blade behind the back, we return home.

Home!.. after a lifetime of war and violence feels like myth, a place spoken of in stories--a memory, but still calls us back..
My wife and son, I long to hold. They call to me.
